About the conference

An interdisciplinary frontier between minds and machines

EIF CogAI 2026 brings together cognitive neuroscience, AI, robotics, psychology, philosophy, and computer science to build sustainable research networks across the UK and EU. The programme is designed to support early-career researchers, foster collaborative funding (ERC, UKRI), and open meaningful industry–academia dialogue.

Interdisciplinary by design

Talks and panels intentionally cross neuroscience, machine learning, HCI, and the philosophy of cognition — not as silos, but as a single research conversation.

Built for early-career scholars

Mentorship sessions, journal-editor panels, and ERC grantee Q&A help PhDs and postdocs translate ideas into publications and competitive funding bids.

UK ⇄ EU partnerships

A deliberate focus on rebuilding research bridges between UK and European institutions, with a hybrid format welcoming international participation.

Honorary Chair

Joint Professor in the Department of Psychiatry, Cambridge, and Chair of Neuroimaging at the Sheffield Institute for Translational Neuroscience. Leads the Artificial Intelligence and Computational Neuroscience Group, combining computational methods with state-of-the-art brain imaging (MEG/EEG, 7T MRI, PET) to understand neurological and psychiatric conditions. Submission requirements

What to include in your abstract

Abstracts must describe ongoing or completed work and follow the format below:

  • Title
  • Authors and affiliations
  • Corresponding author with email
  • Keywords (maximum 6)
  • Abstract (maximum 400 words)
  • Preference: oral & poster, or poster-only

All abstracts undergo peer review. If you cannot attend in person, a pre-recorded video presentation is supported.
